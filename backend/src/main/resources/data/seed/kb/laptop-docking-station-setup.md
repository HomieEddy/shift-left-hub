# Laptop Docking Station Setup Guide

This guide covers how to set up your docking station for maximum productivity with dual monitors and all peripherals.

## Supported Docking Stations

| Model | Connection | Max Displays |
|---|---|---|
| Dell WD19TB | Thunderbolt 3 | 2 x 4K@60Hz |
| Lenovo ThinkPad USB-C Dock | USB-C | 2 x 4K@30Hz |
| HP Thunderbolt Dock G4 | Thunderbolt 4 | 3 x 4K@60Hz |

## Step 1: Physical Setup

1. **Connect the power adapter** to the docking station
2. **Connect monitors** to the docking station:
   - Use **DisplayPort** or **HDMI** cables
   - For dual monitors, use two ports
3. **Connect peripherals:**
   - Keyboard and mouse to USB ports
   - Ethernet cable for wired network
   - Headset to audio jack
4. **Connect your laptop:**
   - Plug the USB-C/Thunderbolt cable into your laptop
   - Wait 5-10 seconds for the docking station to be detected

## Step 2: Configure Displays

### Windows
1. Press `Win + P` and select **Extend**
2. Right-click desktop > **Display Settings**
3. Arrange monitors by dragging them to match your physical layout
4. Set the **primary display** (drag the taskbar to your preferred monitor)
5. Adjust **scale and resolution** if text appears too small

### macOS
1. Go to **System Settings > Displays**
2. Arrange displays by dragging them into position
3. Set the **primary display** by dragging the white menu bar
4. Adjust **resolution** — "Default for display" is usually best

## Step 3: Connect to Network

The Ethernet port on your dock provides a more stable connection than Wi-Fi:
1. Connect an Ethernet cable from the dock to the wall jack
2. Windows/macOS will automatically detect the wired connection
3. Verify connection: look for the **Ethernet** icon in the taskbar/menu bar

## Troubleshooting

### Display Not Detected
- Unplug and replug the monitor cable
- Restart your laptop while connected to the dock
- Update your **docking station firmware** (see step 4)

### USB Devices Not Working
- Disconnect and reconnect the device
- Try a different USB port on the dock
- Update USB drivers via Windows Update

### Laptop Not Charging
- Ensure the dock's power adapter is connected to a working outlet
- Try a different USB-C port on your laptop
- Check that the power adapter is the correct wattage (90W+ recommended)

## Step 4: Firmware Updates

Keep your docking station firmware up to date:
1. **Dell:** [Dell Command | Update](https://www.dell.com/support)
2. **Lenovo:** [Lenovo Dock Manager](https://support.lenovo.com)
3. **HP:** [HP Firmware Packager](https://support.hp.com)

IT recommends checking for firmware updates **quarterly**.
