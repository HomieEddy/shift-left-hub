# Records Retention Schedule

Shift-Left Knowledge Hub maintains a formal records retention schedule to comply with legal, regulatory, and operational requirements while managing storage costs.

## Retention Periods by Category

### Corporate Records
| Record Type | Retention Period | Authority |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Certificate of Incorporation | Permanent | Corporate law |
| Board minutes and resolutions | Permanent | Corporate law |
| Annual reports | Permanent | Securities law |
| Shareholder records | Permanent | Corporate law |
| Corporate seals and stamps | Permanent | Corporate law |

### Financial Records
| Record Type | Retention Period | Authority |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------|
| General ledgers | 7 years | Tax law |
| Accounts payable/receivable | 7 years | Tax law |
| Tax returns and supporting docs | 7 years | Tax law |
| Audit reports | Permanent | Best practice |
| Payroll records | 7 years | Employment law |
| Expense reports | 7 years | Tax law |

### HR Records
| Record Type | Retention Period | Authority |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Employee personnel files | 7 years after termination | Employment law |
| Employment contracts | 7 years after termination | Employment law |
| Benefit enrollment records | 7 years after termination | Benefits law |
| Training records | 3 years after completion | Regulatory |
| I-9 forms | 3 years after hire or 1 year after termination | Immigration law |

### Legal Records
| Record Type | Retention Period | Authority |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Contracts and agreements | 7 years after expiration | Contract law |
| Litigation files | 10 years after case closure | Best practice |
| Patent/trademark filings | Life of IP + 5 years | IP law |
| Insurance policies | 5 years after expiration | Best practice |
| Compliance reports | 7 years | Regulatory |

### Operational Records
| Record Type | Retention Period | Authority |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------|
| IT system logs | 1 year (3 years for security) | Security best practice |
| Customer support tickets | 3 years after resolution | Business/legal |
| Marketing materials | 2 years after last use | Best practice |
| Project documentation | 5 years after project completion | Business |
| Internal communications (email, chat) | 2 years | Business |

## Destruction Procedures

When records reach the end of their retention period:

1. **Review** — Department head or records coordinator reviews records for any pending litigation holds
2. **Approval** — Destruction approved via the Records Destruction Authorization form
3. **Destruction** — Documents destroyed per type:
   - **Physical documents:** Shredded (cross-cut) by approved vendor
   - **Electronic files:** Secure deletion (overwritten, not just "recycle bin")
   - **Backup media:** Destroyed or securely wiped
4. **Certification** — Destruction certificate filed in the records log

## Litigation Hold Exceptions

When a litigation hold is in effect, records covered by the hold must be preserved regardless of their retention schedule (see [Litigation Hold Procedures](/kb/legal-litigation-holds)). The hold overrides the destruction schedule for affected records.

## Retention Schedule Review

This retention schedule is reviewed **annually** by the Legal department and updated as regulations change. Departments are notified of any changes to record categories they manage.

## Non-Compliance

Unauthorized destruction of records may result in disciplinary action and legal penalties. If you are unsure about a record's retention period, do not destroy it — contact `legal@shiftleft-hub.com`.

## Procédures de destruction

Lorsque les documents atteignent la fin de leur période de conservation :

1. **Examen** — Le chef de département examine les documents pour toute mesure conservatoire en cours
2. **Approbation** — Destruction approuvée via le formulaire d'autorisation de destruction
3. **Destruction** — Documents détruits selon le type :
   - **Documents physiques :** Déchiquetés par un fournisseur approuvé
   - **Fichiers électroniques :** Suppression sécurisée (écrasement, pas seulement « corbeille »)
   - **Supports de sauvegarde :** Détruits ou effacés de manière sécurisée
4. **Certification** — Certificat de destruction classé dans le registre

## Exceptions pour mesures conservatoires

Lorsqu'une mesure conservatoire est en vigueur, les documents couverts doivent être préservés indépendamment de leur calendrier de conservation. La mesure prévaut sur le calendrier pour les documents concernés.

## Examen du calendrier

Ce calendrier est examiné **annuellement** par le service juridique et mis à jour en fonction des changements réglementaires. Les départements sont informés de tout changement concernant les catégories qu'ils gèrent.

## Non-conformité

La destruction non autorisée de documents peut entraîner des mesures disciplinaires et des sanctions juridiques. Si vous n'êtes pas sûr de la durée de conservation d'un document, ne le détruisez pas — contactez `juridique@shiftleft-hub.com`.
